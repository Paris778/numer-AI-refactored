# Numerai Architecture Overview

This document provides a structured, high-level technical summary of the Numerai ecosystem, designed for LLM consumption and quick reference.

---

## 1. Core Architecture: The Numerai Triangle

Numerai operates through three interdependent components: the **Hedge Fund**, the **User Models**, and the **Rewards/Staking System**.

### A. The Hedge Fund (HF)

* **Nature:** A traditional, regulated quantitative hedge fund (USA-based).
* **Strategy:** Market neutral (aims for profit regardless of market direction by balancing long and short positions).
* **Operational Role:** The HF executes trades in global stock markets based on the **Meta Model** (the aggregate of user predictions).
* **Data Disclosure:** Performance data and specific holdings are generally private/non-disclosed due to SEC regulations.

### B. User Models (The AI Layer)

Numerai crowdsources stock market predictions (signals) from global data scientists to replace a traditional in-house "army of quants."

* **The Meta Model:** An ensemble model created by combining all staked user submissions. The Hedge Fund’s capital is controlled by this aggregate signal.
* **Participation Tracks:**
* **Numerai Tournament (Classic):** Users receive obfuscated, regularized data. You do not know which features or stocks you are modeling (Black Box). This prevents data licensing issues and forces a pure machine learning approach over financial domain knowledge.
* **Numerai Signals:** For advanced users who bring their own data. Users submit predictions for known stock tickers.


* **Model Requirements:** * Models must **rank** stocks relatively rather than predicting absolute prices.
* Predictions are submitted weekly.
* Numerai never sees your code/model; they only receive the numerical output (predictions).



### C. Rewards & Staking (The Crypto Layer)

The incentive mechanism ensures "skin in the game" to prevent bad actors and identify high-confidence signals.

* **Numeraire (NMR):** An ERC-20 token on the Ethereum blockchain used for staking.
* **Staking Mechanism:**
* **Optional:** Users can submit models without staking to learn/test. Unstaked models do not earn rewards or influence the Meta Model.
* **Skin in the Game:** To earn rewards, users lock NMR to their model.
* **The "Burn":** If a model's correlation with the target is negative (poor performance), a portion of the staked NMR is **destroyed** (erased from the blockchain).
* **The "Earn":** If a model's correlation is positive, the user earns additional NMR.


* **Erasure Protocol:** The decentralized smart contract framework that handles the locking and destruction of NMR.
* **Key Distinction:** When NMR is burned, it is not "taken" by Numerai; it is permanently removed from the total supply.

## 2. Technical Concepts for Data Scientists

| Concept | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Obfuscated Data** | Feature names and stock identities are hidden. Models must find patterns in the abstract math. |
| **Eras** | Data is organized into temporal blocks. While rows are independent, they belong to specific time periods. |
| **Correlation (Corr)** | The primary scoring metric. Measures how well your predicted rankings align with actual market outcomes. |
| **MMC (Meta Model Contribution)** | An optional score measuring how much value your model adds to the Meta Model ensemble relative to other users. |
| **Stake-Weighting** | The Meta Model gives more weight to users who have staked more NMR, assuming stake size correlates with user confidence. |

## 3. Workflow Summary

1. **Download:** Access the obfuscated dataset from the Tournament page.
2. **Train:** Build a machine learning model (Regression/Ranking) to predict the target.
3. **Submit:** Upload predictions to the Numerai API/Website.
4. **Stake:** (Optional) Lock NMR to the model.
5. **Score:** Wait for real-world market results (scoring happens over several weeks).
6. **Payout:** Receive NMR rewards or suffer a "burn" based on correlation performance.
